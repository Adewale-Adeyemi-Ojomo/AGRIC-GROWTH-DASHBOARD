# AGRIC-GROWTH-DASHBOARD
Built a data-driven AgricTech dashboard showcasing how technology can improve yield, streamline harvest cycles, and enhance distribution efficiency across 10 pilot states in Nigeria.

##Executive Summary

This project analyzes an AgricTech model optimizing farm production, harvest, and distribution across 10 Nigerian states, using data from 21,000+ farmers.

Findings show a profitable and resilient system ($57M+ revenue, 50% margin) with key opportunities in reducing post-harvest losses, improving technology adoption, and enhancing soil health.

Insight: AgricTech is positioned as a scalable, data-driven platform for improving agricultural productivity and efficiency.

###Key Insights from the AgricTech Dashboard
# 1. Significant Revenue Leakage from Post-Harvest Losses
Post-harvest losses amount to $187,320, highlighting a major inefficiency in storage and logistics.
Insight: Reducing losses presents an immediate opportunity to improve profitability and recover lost revenue.

# 2. Large and Scalable Market Opportunity
The platform currently supports 21,000 farmers across multiple states, within a sector contributing over 25% of Nigeria’s GDP.
Insight: The scale of agriculture in Nigeria presents strong growth potential for expansion and technology adoption.

# 3. Consistent Revenue Growth Indicates Market Demand
Revenue grew from $10.9M (2021) to $11.57M (2025), showing steady demand despite industry challenges.
Insight: The business demonstrates resilience and consistent market relevance.

# 4. Diversified Revenue Streams Reduce Risk
Revenue is generated across 8 crop types and 5 off-taker channels, led by Government Agencies ($13.33M).
Insight: Diversification protects the business from market, pricing, and demand shocks.

 # 5. Data as a Competitive Advantage
Access to real-time data across farmers, crops, soil health, and carbon tracking provides deeper operational insights.
Insight: Data-driven decision-making creates a strong competitive moat over traditional agribusiness models.

# 6. Strong Profitability and Efficient Cost Structure
The business maintains a 50.19% profit margin, which is high for the agriculture sector.
Insight: Efficient operations and pricing strategies support sustainable profitability.

# 7. Low Technology Adoption = High Growth Opportunity
Technology adoption score is 4.3/10, with leading adoption in Kano (793 farmers).
Insight: Increasing adoption directly correlates with improved yield, efficiency, and revenue growth.

# 8. Yield Performance Reflects Untapped Potential

Average yield stands at 4 tonnes/hectare, aligned with national averages.
Insight: There is significant room to improve productivity through technology and better farming practices.

# 9. Soil Health Indicates Opportunity for Yield Improvement
Soil health index is 40/80, showing moderate conditions.
Insight: Improving soil quality can directly increase yield and overall farm output.

# 10. Climate Risk is Managed Through Diversification

Operations span multiple crops, states, and seasons.
Insight: Diversification reduces exposure to climate variability and ensures business continuity.

# Overall Insight

The AgricTech model is profitable, resilient, and scalable, with its biggest opportunities lying in:

Reducing post-harvest losses
Increasing technology adoption
Improving soil health and yield

# Conclusion:
This is not just a farming system—it is a data-driven agricultural optimization platform with strong potential for scale.
